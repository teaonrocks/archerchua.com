As a freelance audio engineer, I dreaded the admin work after finishing a mix: formatting spreadsheets, calculating totals, exporting PDFs, and tracking payments. Existing tools felt overkill and expensive for a solo freelancer, so I built **InvoiceThing**, a focused tool that generates PDF invoices and tracks their status.

Repository: https://github.com/teaonrocks/invoicething

## What It Does

InvoiceThing is a lightweight web app that lets me:

- Create invoices with line items, tax, and totals
- Generate clean PDFs automatically
- Track payment status (paid, pending, overdue)
- Store client information for reuse
- View all invoices in a simple dashboard

That's deliberately it. No time tracking, no expense management, no complex reporting. I can create an invoice in under two minutes.

## Design Rationale

**Why build it?** Excel templates were manual and error-prone. QuickBooks and similar tools were designed for teams and cost too much for my needs. Building it myself gave me full control and let me practise full-stack development on a real problem.

**Why keep it simple?** I deliberately avoided feature creep. The scope is narrow: create an invoice, generate a PDF, mark it as paid. This makes it fast to use and easy to maintain.

**Technical choices:** Next.js and TypeScript for the framework, Convex for the database (simpler than managing a separate server), and Tailwind CSS for quick, professional styling. PDF generation is handled by a library that eliminates the manual "save as PDF" step I hated.

## Challenges & Learning

**The fear of real money:** Sending my first real invoice through InvoiceThing was intimidating. What if the PDF was wrong or the totals were off? I triple-checked everything, sent it, and held my breath. It worked. The client paid. That validation that I had built something that handles real money end-to-end was more rewarding than any school assignment.

**Client data management:** I initially over-engineered a full CRM, then realised I only needed to store essential info (name, email). Simplifying reduced complexity and kept the app lightweight.

## Conclusion

InvoiceThing is not revolutionary software, but it solves my problem precisely. Building it taught me that the best tools often come from scratching your own itch; when you experience a problem first-hand, you understand the nuances that generic solutions miss.

InvoiceThing started as a way to avoid spreadsheet hell and became proof that I could design, build, and trust a small product that handles real money, real clients, and real business needs.
