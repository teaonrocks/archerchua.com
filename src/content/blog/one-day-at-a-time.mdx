## Overview

One Day at a Time is a poverty simulation experience built to help participants understand how quickly small decisions can compound when resources are limited. The web app supports a live, Kahoot-style session where facilitators can guide a room through scenarios while participants respond from their own devices.

The goal was to make the experience feel immediate without making the technology the focus. Participants needed clear prompts, fast feedback, and a reliable way to stay in sync with the facilitator.

## What I Built

The project uses React, Next.js, Convex, and Tailwind to support real-time gameplay. I worked on the interaction flow, participant experience, and facilitator-facing screens so the simulation could move quickly between questions, explanations, and results.

Convex handled the real-time state changes, which made it easier to keep every participant aligned during the session. Tailwind helped keep the interface consistent while we iterated on the layout and pacing.

## What I Learned

This project reinforced how important facilitation context is when building group software. A screen can be technically correct and still fail if it slows down the person running the room.

The strongest parts of the app came from designing around the live experience: short prompts, obvious states, and fewer chances for participants to get lost.
